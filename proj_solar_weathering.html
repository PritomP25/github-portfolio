☀️ Solar Weathering Station

A Renewable-Powered Environmental Monitoring Platform

  • Type: Analog Circuit Project
  • Core: 1st/2nd Order Filters + solar power harvesting
  • Goal: Monitor environmental conditions affecting material weathering

This project was a joint effort with me, Andy Huang, and Eric Lin.

Jump to Demo

Overview

The Solar Weathering Station integrates solar energy harvesting, sensor data acquisition, and circuit components like op-amps and filters. It continuously monitors environmental factors like temperature, humidity, light intensity, and UV exposure to study material weathering effects in outdoor conditions.

Technical Breakdown

Power Management

Designed a solar harvesting circuit with regulator stages to ensure stable 5V/3.3V operation. Energy stored in a capacitor or battery provides power during cloudy periods, verified via LTSpice efficiency analysis.

Data Acquisition

Sensors continuously sampled by Op-Amps. Thresholds set for each nature's exposure for allowing certain information to be pass thru via their designed filters. Data shown on LTspice and logged via serial for long-term studies.

System Operation
  1. Idle: Low power mode when sunlight is weak. Use energy from solar panel.
  2. Active: Solar harvesting energy from capacitor to power sensors.

Challenges & Trade-offs

  • Ensuring energy balance between harvesting and load.
  • Choosing storage capacity for cloudy conditions.
  • Calibrating sensors to handle real-world fluctuations.
  • Managing solar panel to sensor with necessarily power consumption

Future Improvements

  • Wireless communication (LoRa/Wi-Fi) for remote data upload.
  • Upgrade to low-power MCUs (Arduino, ESP32-S2, STM32L series).
  • Data logging to SD card for extended studies.
  • Use larger solar arrays or supercapacitors for reliability.

What I Learned

  • Practical solar energy harvesting and regulation techniques.
  • Integration of multiple sensors via analog persective.
  • Uses of op-amps and 1st/2nd order filters.
  • Designing for outdoor reliability and environmental resilience.
